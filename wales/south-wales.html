South Wales

Castles & Historic Sites

Cardiff · Swansea · Vale of Glamorgan · Bridgend · Neath Port Talbot · Caerphilly · Merthyr Tydfil · Newport · Torfaen · Monmouthshire

South Wales packs an extraordinary density of castles into the landscape between Swansea and the English border. This region is home to 54 castles, abbeys, and historic sites — from the lavish Victorian interiors of Cardiff Castle and the fairy-tale turrets of Castell Coch to the mighty Caerphilly Castle with its famous leaning tower. Follow the Wye Valley to discover the romantic ruins of Tintern Abbey, the imposing Chepstow Castle — the oldest surviving post-Roman stone fortification in Britain — and the magnificent Raglan Castle with its Great Tower. From Roman legions at Caerleon to Norman conquerors in the Vale of Glamorgan, South Wales tells the story of conquest, resistance, and reinvention.

Getting There

Travelling to South Wales

South Wales is the most accessible part of Wales. The M4 motorway runs directly from London to Cardiff (2 hours), Swansea (3 hours), and connects to the entire south coast. The Severn Bridge (M48) and Prince of Wales Bridge (M4) cross from England — both toll-free since 2018.

By train, Great Western Railway runs frequent high-speed services from London Paddington to Cardiff Central in under 2 hours. Transport for Wales connects Cardiff to Swansea, Newport, and the valleys. Cardiff also has an international airport with flights to European destinations.

Within South Wales, the rail and bus network is decent — you can reach Cardiff Castle, Caerphilly, and Chepstow by public transport. However, a car opens up the Wye Valley, Vale of Glamorgan coast, and valley castles that are harder to reach otherwise.

Accommodation

Where to Stay in South Wales

Cardiff is the obvious base — Wales's capital city has everything from luxury hotels to budget hostels, plus excellent restaurants, nightlife, and culture. It's perfectly placed for Cardiff Castle, Castell Coch, and Caerphilly, with easy rail connections to the wider region.

For the Wye Valley and Monmouthshire castles, Chepstow, Monmouth, or Abergavenny make excellent bases. Abergavenny in particular has become a foodie destination, with outstanding restaurants and gastro-pubs. The Wye Valley has charming B&Bs and country house hotels.

Swansea and the Gower Peninsula are ideal for combining castle visits with beach days — the Gower was Britain's first Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty. For something special, The Celtic Manor near Newport and several country house hotels in Monmouthshire offer luxury stays.

Planning

Best Time to Visit South Wales Castles

South Wales is great year-round thanks to its relatively mild climate and the fact that many of its best sites — Cardiff Castle, Caerphilly, Chepstow — are well set up for visitors in all seasons. Summer (June–August) brings the warmest weather and events like the Cardiff Castle Summer Concert Series.

Spring (April–May) is wonderful — bluebells in the Wye Valley woods, wildflowers along the Glamorgan coast, and fewer crowds at popular sites. The Six Nations rugby in February/March makes Cardiff buzzing and atmospheric, though book accommodation early.

Autumn (September–October) is arguably the best time for the Wye Valley — the river valley ablaze with colour is one of Britain's finest autumn landscapes, and Tintern Abbey framed by golden beech trees is unforgettable. Winter is quiet but Cardiff Castle's Christmas events and the atmospheric ruins on crisp winter days have their own appeal.

Frequently Asked

South Wales Castle FAQ

How many castles are in South Wales?
There are around 54 castles, ruins, abbeys, and historic sites across South Wales. The exact count on CastleCore is 54, though new sites are added regularly. South Wales has one of the highest concentrations of castles anywhere in Europe.
What is the most famous castle in South Wales?
Cardiff Castle is the most famous, sitting in the heart of the Welsh capital with Roman foundations, a Norman keep, and spectacular Victorian Gothic apartments. Caerphilly Castle — the largest castle in Wales and second largest in Britain — is equally iconic with its famous leaning tower and vast water defences.
Is Chepstow Castle worth visiting?
Absolutely. Chepstow Castle is the oldest surviving post-Roman stone fortification in Britain, begun in 1067 just a year after the Norman Conquest. Its dramatic cliff-top position above the River Wye and the massive Great Tower make it one of the most impressive castles in Wales. Allow at least 90 minutes.
Can I visit Cardiff Castle and Caerphilly Castle in one day?
Yes — they're only 20 minutes apart by car or 30 minutes by train plus a short walk. You could easily combine both in a day trip, with Castell Coch (15 minutes north of Cardiff) as a bonus. Allow 2 hours for Cardiff Castle and 1.5 hours for Caerphilly.
What else is there to see in South Wales besides castles?
South Wales offers the Brecon Beacons National Park, the Gower Peninsula beaches, the historic Wye Valley, Tintern Abbey, the Big Pit coal mining museum, Cardiff Bay waterfront, the Millennium Centre for performing arts, and excellent food scenes in Cardiff and Abergavenny. The region also has a rich industrial heritage with fascinating museums.

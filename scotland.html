The Treasury

Artifacts & Treasures

Scotland's most precious relics — crown jewels, Viking hoards, and medieval masterpieces.

The Honours of Scotland

Edinburgh Castle

The oldest crown jewels in Britain — crown, sceptre, and sword of state — hidden from Cromwell inside Dunnottar Castle's walls.

The Stone of Destiny

Edinburgh Castle (returned 1996)

The coronation stone of Scottish kings, seized by Edward I in 1296 and famously stolen back by Scottish students in 1950.

The Lewis Chessmen

British Museum / National Museum of Scotland

93 twelfth-century walrus ivory chess pieces found on the Isle of Lewis in 1831. Among the most iconic medieval artifacts in the world.

The Monymusk Reliquary

National Museum of Scotland

Believed to have contained a relic of St Columba and carried into the Battle of Bannockburn as a holy talisman.

The Hunterston Brooch

National Museum of Scotland

A magnificent 8th-century Celtic/Viking brooch — one of the finest examples of early medieval metalwork in Europe.

⚔️

The Galloway Hoard

National Museum of Scotland

A Viking-age treasure trove discovered in 2014 — the richest ever found in Scotland, with gold, silver, glass, and textiles from across the medieval world.

Living Heritage

Culture & Traditions

Tartan, whisky, Highland games — the living traditions that give Scotland's castles their soul.

Tartan & Clans

The visual language of Scottish identity. Clan tartans, Highland dress, the kilt — each pattern tells a family's story.

Whisky

Scotland's 130+ active distilleries, from Islay peat monsters to Speyside honey. Many occupy former castle estates.

Highland Games

Caber toss, stone put, Highland dancing. Held at castle grounds across Scotland since the days of the early kings.

Hogmanay & Festivals

Edinburgh's world-famous New Year, Up Helly Aa's Viking fire festival, Burns Night — Scotland celebrates like nowhere else.

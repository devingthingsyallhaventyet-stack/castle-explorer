## 2. Detailed Notes Per Site

### 2.1 VisitBritain.com / VisitEngland.com
**Type:** National tourism boards  
**Strengths:**
- Modern, polished design with strong brand
- Good high-level editorial content for "history and heritage"
- Itineraries and themed content (Bridgerton locations, Peaky Blinders, etc.)
- Multi-language support
- Links to VisitBritain Shop for tickets

**Weaknesses:**
- No dedicated castle listing page (404 on /castles URLs)
- Heritage content is editorial/inspirational, not a searchable database
- No map browsing, no filters for castle type/era/condition
- No user reviews or community features
- Cherry-picks only "hero" sites â€” no comprehensive coverage
- VisitEngland similarly organized by "interest" not by site type
- No transport or accessibility info on heritage pages

**Castle enthusiast verdict:** Useless for planning a castle-focused trip. Good for inspiration but zero depth.

---

### 2.2 VisitScotland.com
**Type:** National tourism board (Scotland)  
**Strengths:**
- **Dedicated castles category** with proper listing page
- Browse by region, extensive geographic taxonomy
- Beta map feature for discovering sites
- "Stay in a castle" accommodation category â€” smart cross-sell
- Itineraries and touring routes
- UNESCO Trail content
- Well-organized navigation with castles as a first-class category
- Multi-language (6 languages)

**Weaknesses:**
- Castle pages are listings/links, not deep content
- No filters by era, condition (ruin vs intact), or admission type
- Map is labeled "beta" â€” still rough
- No user reviews
- No cross-linking between nearby castles
- Limited to VisitScotland-listed properties (not every ruin)
- No transport directions per site

**Castle enthusiast verdict:** Best of the national tourism boards for castles. Good starting point but lacks depth for serious enthusiasts.

- No user reviews
- No real route planning between sites
- No transport directions (just addresses)
- No era/period filter
- No "condition" filter (ruin vs intact vs partially restored)
- Pages are functional but not inspiring â€” focus on visitor info over storytelling
- No interactive map with route planning

**Castle enthusiast verdict:** The most functional castle browsing experience, but limited to their own ~70 castles. The nearby/similar cross-linking is the best feature any site offers.

National Trust = NT properties only. Nobody covers ALL castles across the UK and Ireland in one place. A tourist driving from Edinburgh to Dublin literally needs 5+ different websites.

**Scenic Route opportunity:** The ONLY unified castle database spanning England, Scotland, Wales, Northern Ireland, and the Republic of Ireland.

- **Castle type** (motte-and-bailey, concentric, tower house, fortified manor)

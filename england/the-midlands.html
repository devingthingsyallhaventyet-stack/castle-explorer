The Midlands

The Midlands — the heart of England — is a region where medieval power, industrial revolution, and pastoral beauty collide. Across twelve counties, from the Welsh Marches to the Lincolnshire fens, 0 castles and historic sites chart a turbulent thousand-year history. Warwick Castle, towering above the River Avon, is one of England's mightiest medieval fortresses and most visited historic sites. Nearby, the vast ruins of Kenilworth Castle — where Elizabeth I was lavishly entertained by Robert Dudley — sprawl across acres of Warwickshire countryside. On the Welsh border, Ludlow Castle commands its hilltop in one of England's finest medieval towns, while Goodrich Castle guards the Wye Valley in Herefordshire. In the east, Lincoln Castle holds one of only four surviving copies of Magna Carta, and Bolsover Castle perches on its Derbyshire ridge like something from a Stuart fantasy. From Shakespeare country to Robin Hood's Nottinghamshire, the Midlands delivers castle after castle.

Getting There

Travelling to The Midlands

The Midlands sits at the centre of England's transport network, making it one of the easiest regions to reach. Birmingham, England's second city, has an international airport with flights across Europe and beyond, plus Birmingham New Street — the country's busiest interchange station — with trains from London Euston in just 1 hour 20 minutes by Avanti West Coast.

For the eastern Midlands, East Midlands Parkway and Nottingham station are well-served by trains from London St Pancras (EMR, around 1.5–2 hours). Lincoln is connected via Newark and Nottingham. The M1, M6, M5, and M40 motorways all converge on the region, making car travel straightforward from any direction.

Within the Midlands, a car is the most flexible option for castle-hopping, especially in rural Shropshire, Herefordshire, and the Lincolnshire countryside. However, major sites like Warwick Castle (Warwick station, 2-minute walk) and Lincoln Castle (Lincoln station, 15-minute walk uphill) are very accessible by public transport. Stratford-upon-Avon has its own rail line from Birmingham.

Accommodation

Where to Stay in The Midlands

Warwick and Stratford-upon-Avon make an excellent combined base for the western Midlands — charming market towns with a strong range of B&Bs, coaching inns, and boutique hotels. You're within easy striking distance of Warwick Castle, Kenilworth, and the Cotswolds fringe.

For the Welsh Marches, Ludlow is one of England's finest food towns — a medieval gem with Michelin-starred restaurants, independent shops, and a castle at its heart. Hereford and Shrewsbury are larger alternatives with more accommodation options. In the east, Lincoln's uphill cathedral quarter has atmospheric B&Bs and hotels, while Nottingham offers a lively city base.

For something special, several Midlands castles and stately homes operate as hotels. Ettington Park near Stratford (a Gothic Victorian manor), Coombe Abbey near Coventry, and various country house hotels in the Shropshire hills offer the chance to sleep in historic surroundings. The region also has excellent glamping and cottage rental options, particularly in the Herefordshire countryside and Peak District fringe.

Planning

Best Time to Visit Midlands Castles

April to October is the main season, with all sites open and the countryside at its best. The Midlands has a relatively mild climate by English standards — sheltered from coastal weather, it's often a degree or two warmer than northern or western regions.

May and June are ideal: long days, gardens in full bloom (the Midlands is rose country), and the Shakespeare season in full swing at Stratford. September brings harvest festivals, golden light, and thinning crowds — arguably the best month for photography at castle sites.

Winter is perfectly viable for Midlands castle visits. Warwick Castle runs spectacular Christmas events (November–January), and Lincoln's Christmas Market is one of England's largest and most atmospheric. Many English Heritage ruins stay open year-round. The Midlands rarely sees heavy snow, so access is seldom an issue — though check individual site opening times as some smaller properties close November to March.

Frequently Asked

Midlands Castle FAQ

How many castles are in The Midlands?
The Midlands has one of the highest concentrations of castles in England, spanning twelve counties from Lincolnshire in the east to Shropshire on the Welsh border. The region includes everything from mighty medieval fortresses to Elizabethan manor houses and Civil War strongholds. The exact count on CastleCore is 0, with new sites added regularly.
What is the most famous castle in The Midlands?
Warwick Castle is the Midlands' most visited and famous castle — a spectacular medieval fortress on the banks of the River Avon that's been welcoming visitors for over a thousand years. Now operated by Merlin Entertainments, it combines genuine medieval history with family-friendly attractions, jousting displays, and seasonal events.
Is Warwick Castle worth visiting?
Absolutely. Warwick Castle is one of England's finest medieval fortresses, with over 1,100 years of history. The castle offers a mix of historical exhibits, interactive experiences, dungeon tours, birds of prey displays, and spectacular grounds on the River Avon. Allow at least half a day — there's enough to fill a full day, especially with children. Book tickets online in advance for the best prices (significant savings over gate prices).
What are the best free castles in The Midlands?
Several notable Midlands castles are free to visit or have free exterior access. English Heritage members can visit Kenilworth, Goodrich, and Bolsover at no extra cost. Lincoln Castle's wall walk requires a ticket, but the grounds offer great views for free. Many smaller castle ruins and earthworks across the region — such as those at Clun, Wigmore, and Groby — are freely accessible in open countryside.
Can you combine castle visits with Shakespeare country?
Yes — Warwick Castle and Kenilworth Castle are both within 15 minutes' drive of Stratford-upon-Avon. You can easily combine a morning at one of the castles with an afternoon exploring Shakespeare's birthplace, Anne Hathaway's Cottage, and the Royal Shakespeare Theatre. It's one of the best day trips in the Midlands and among the most popular visitor circuits in England.

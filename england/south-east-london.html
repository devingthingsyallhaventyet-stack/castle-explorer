South East & London

Castles & Historic Sites

Kent · East Sussex · West Sussex · Surrey · Hampshire · Berkshire · Buckinghamshire · Oxfordshire · London

South East England & London is the castle heartland of the nation — the region where William the Conqueror first stamped Norman authority on English soil. The Tower of London, founded in 1066, remains the most visited castle in Britain, while Dover Castle has guarded the Channel crossing for nearly a thousand years. In Kent's rolling countryside, Leeds Castle floats on its lake like a scene from a fairy tale, and Hever Castle whispers of Anne Boleyn's childhood. Windsor Castle, the world's oldest and largest occupied castle, has been the home of monarchs since Henry I. Along the Sussex coast, the picture-perfect Bodiam Castle and the great fortress of Arundel Castle complete a region that offers more castles per square mile than almost anywhere in the world. From Roman forts to Tudor palaces, this is where England's story was written in stone.

Getting There

Travelling to South East England & London

This is the most accessible castle region in Britain. London is the natural gateway — served by six international airports, Eurostar from Paris and Brussels, and the UK's busiest rail network. The Tower of London is a short walk from Tower Hill tube station.

By train, Kent's castles are superbly connected: High Speed 1 reaches Canterbury in under an hour from St Pancras, and Dover in just over an hour. Windsor is 30 minutes from Paddington or Waterloo. Arundel and the Sussex coast are about 90 minutes from Victoria.

By car, the M25 orbital motorway connects all the major routes into the region — the M20/A20 to Kent, M23/A23 to Sussex, M3 to Hampshire, and M4/M40 to Berkshire and Oxfordshire. Parking is available at most castle sites, though London itself is best explored by public transport.

Accommodation

Where to Stay in the South East

London offers endless accommodation options, from budget hostels to luxury hotels — and many castles are easy day trips from the capital. For a more immersive experience, Canterbury is a beautiful medieval city and perfect base for Kent's castles.

In Sussex, the charming towns of Lewes, Rye, and Arundel itself offer atmospheric B&Bs and boutique hotels within walking distance of castle sites. Winchester — England's ancient capital — is an excellent Hampshire base.

For a castle stay, Leeds Castle has glamping and holiday cottages in its grounds, Amberley Castle in Sussex operates as a luxury hotel, and Hever Castle offers B&B accommodation in its Astor Wing. There's nothing quite like waking up inside castle walls with the morning mist lifting over the moat.

Planning

Best Time to Visit South East Castles

April to October is the main season, with the longest days and warmest weather. The South East enjoys more sunshine than any other part of England, and most castle gardens are at their finest from May to July.

Spring (April–May) brings bluebell woods around many castle grounds and manageable crowds. Summer is peak season — expect queues at the Tower of London and Windsor, but living history events, jousting tournaments, and open-air theatre make the buzz worthwhile. Book timed tickets in advance for the most popular sites.

Autumn and winter have their own appeal. October half-term brings Halloween events at many castles. Christmas is spectacular — Leeds Castle, Hever, and many others host festive markets and illuminated trails. The Tower of London and Windsor Castle are open year-round and are far less crowded in winter months.

Frequently Asked

South East & London Castle FAQ

How many castles are in South East England and London?
South East England and London contain one of the densest concentrations of castles in Britain. The exact count on CastleCore is 0, spanning from Norman keeps and medieval fortresses to Tudor palaces and Civil War strongholds.
What is the most famous castle in South East England?
The Tower of London is the most famous — a UNESCO World Heritage Site founded by William the Conqueror in 1066. It houses the Crown Jewels and nearly 1,000 years of royal history. Outside London, Dover Castle ("Key to England") and Windsor Castle (the world's oldest occupied castle) are equally renowned.
Which South East castles are free to visit?
Many castle ruins across the South East are free to visit, including Pevensey Castle, Eynsford Castle, and numerous earthwork sites. Major attractions like the Tower of London (£33), Windsor Castle (£30), and Leeds Castle (£28) charge admission but are well worth it. English Heritage and Historic Royal Palaces memberships offer great value for repeat visitors.
What is the best castle in Kent?
Kent is spoilt for choice. Leeds Castle, set on two islands in a lake, is regularly voted the world's most beautiful castle. Dover Castle dominates the White Cliffs with massive medieval fortifications and secret wartime tunnels. Hever Castle, childhood home of Anne Boleyn, offers stunning Tudor history and Italian gardens. It depends on whether you prefer grandeur, history, or romance.
Can I visit South East castles as day trips from London?
Absolutely — this is one of the best regions for castle day trips. Windsor Castle is 30 minutes by train from Paddington. Leeds Castle, Dover, Bodiam, and Arundel are all reachable within 1–2 hours by train or car. The Tower of London is right in the city centre at Tower Hill. You could easily visit a different castle every weekend for months.

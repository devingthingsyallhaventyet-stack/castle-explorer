South West

Castles & Historic Sites

Cornwall · Devon · Dorset · Somerset · Wiltshire · Gloucestershire · Isle of Wight

The South West is where England meets the Atlantic — a land of dramatic coastlines, rolling moorland, and castles steeped in legend. On Cornwall's wild north coast, Tintagel Castle clings to sea cliffs shrouded in Arthurian myth, while the tidal island of St Michael's Mount rises like a fairy tale from Mount's Bay. In Dorset, the shattered towers of Corfe Castle crown a hilltop that has witnessed sieges, betrayals, and a thousand years of English history. Somerset's Dunster Castle gazes across Exmoor to the Bristol Channel, and Berkeley Castle in Gloucestershire — where Edward II met his grim end in 1327 — remains one of England's oldest continuously inhabited castles. On the Isle of Wight, Carisbrooke Castle held Charles I prisoner before his execution. From Iron Age hillforts to Civil War battlegrounds, every corner of the South West tells a story written in ancient stone.

Getting There

Travelling to the South West

The Great Western Railway mainline from London Paddington is the backbone of South West travel. Exeter is reached in around 2 hours, Plymouth in 3 hours, and Penzance — gateway to Cornwall's castles — in about 5 hours. The journey along the sea wall at Dawlish is one of Britain's most spectacular rail stretches.

By car, the M5 motorway runs from Bristol to Exeter, where it meets the A30/A38 continuing into Devon and Cornwall. The M4 from London reaches Bristol in under 2 hours. Allow extra time in summer — the A30 through Cornwall can be slow during peak holiday weeks.

The Isle of Wight is reached by ferry from Portsmouth, Southampton, or Lymington — crossings take 20–60 minutes depending on the route. Exeter and Bristol airports serve the region with domestic and European flights. Once in the South West, a car is strongly recommended — distances between castle sites can be significant, and rural bus services are limited.

Accommodation

Where to Stay in the South West

Exeter is an excellent central base — a lively cathedral city with good rail links and easy access to both Devon and Dorset castles. Bath makes a luxurious base for the northern South West, combining world-class Georgian architecture with proximity to Gloucestershire and Wiltshire sites.

For Cornwall, Bodmin and Truro are well-positioned for reaching both coasts. The fishing villages of St Ives, Padstow, and Fowey offer charming coastal accommodation within reach of castle sites. In Dorset, Wareham is perfectly placed for Corfe Castle — just a short drive or heritage railway ride away.

For castle stays, Taunton Castle operates as a hotel in the heart of Somerset, and several National Trust properties in the region offer holiday cottages on castle estates. The South West also excels in glamping, yurts, and shepherd's huts — many with castle views. Book well ahead for summer in Cornwall and Dorset.

Planning

Best Time to Visit South West Castles

May to September is peak season, with the warmest weather and longest days. The South West benefits from the Gulf Stream, making it milder than much of England — Cornwall's coast can feel almost Mediterranean in a good summer. The trade-off is crowds: roads and popular sites are very busy in July and August school holidays.

Late April, May, and September are the sweet spot — warm enough for comfortable exploring, long daylight hours, but far fewer crowds. Coastal wildflowers are at their peak in May, and the light in September is golden. Most castle interiors are open throughout this period.

Autumn and winter bring a different kind of magic. Storm-watching from clifftop castle ruins is unforgettable — Tintagel in a gale is genuinely awe-inspiring. Corfe Castle is atmospheric in fog. Many interiors close from November to March, but exterior ruins are accessible year-round. Christmas events run at several National Trust castle properties.

Frequently Asked

South West Castle FAQ

How many castles are in South West England?
South West England has a rich collection of castles and historic fortifications spread across Cornwall, Devon, Dorset, Somerset, Wiltshire, Gloucestershire, and the Isle of Wight. The exact count on CastleCore is 0, ranging from dramatic clifftop ruins to perfectly preserved medieval strongholds.
What is the most famous castle in the South West?
Tintagel Castle in Cornwall is the most famous, perched on dramatic sea cliffs and forever linked with the legend of King Arthur. Corfe Castle in Dorset — its shattered towers crowning a natural hilltop — is equally iconic and one of England's most photographed ruins. St Michael's Mount, rising from Mount's Bay on a tidal island, completes the trio of unmissable sites.
Is Tintagel Castle really connected to King Arthur?
Tintagel has been associated with King Arthur since Geoffrey of Monmouth's 12th-century writings, which named it as Arthur's birthplace. Archaeological excavations have revealed a high-status Dark Age settlement dating to the 5th–6th centuries — the right period for the historical Arthur — though no definitive link has been proven. English Heritage's dramatic bridge to the headland, opened in 2019, adds to the sense of stepping into legend.
What is the best time to visit South West castles?
May to September offers the best weather, with long daylight hours and all sites open. The South West benefits from the Gulf Stream, making it milder than much of England. Late spring and early autumn are ideal for avoiding peak holiday crowds while enjoying warm weather. Many coastal castles are spectacular in autumn storms — Tintagel in a gale is unforgettable.
Can I reach South West castles by public transport?
The Great Western Railway mainline runs from London Paddington to Exeter, Plymouth, and Penzance, passing near many castle sites. Corfe Castle has its own heritage railway station on the Swanage Railway. However, the more remote Cornish and Devon coast castles — particularly Tintagel — are best reached by car. Local buses connect major towns but services can be infrequent in rural areas.

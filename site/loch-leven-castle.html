About

The island castle where Mary, Queen of Scots was imprisoned and forced to abdicate in 1567. She escaped by boat after nearly a year. Accessible only by ferry.

Lochleven Castle is a ruined castle on an island in Loch Leven, in the Perth and Kinross local authority area of Scotland. Possibly built around 1300, the castle was the site of military action during the Wars of Scottish Independence (1296â€“1357). In the latter part of the 14th century, the castle was granted to William Douglas, 1st Earl of Douglas, by his uncle. It remained in the Douglases' hands for the next 300 years.

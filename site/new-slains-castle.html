New Slains Castle

📍 Aberdeenshire, Scotland

4.2 ★★★★ 1,600 reviews

About

A dramatic clifftop ruin where the wild North Sea crashes below roofless halls — said to have inspired Bram Stoker's vision of Dracula's castle during his visits to Cruden Bay.

Built in 1597 by the 9th Earl of Erroll after the original Slains Castle was destroyed by James VI. Extended significantly in the 19th century into a grand baronial mansion. Bram Stoker was a regular visitor to nearby Cruden Bay from 1892-1910 and is believed to have drawn inspiration for Dracula from the castle's dramatic clifftop setting. The roof was removed in 1925 for tax purposes and it has been a ruin since.

About

The childhood home of Anne Boleyn, Hever Castle was lavishly restored by William Waldorf Astor in the early 1900s. Its moated grounds include an Italian Garden and Tudor village.

Hever Castle is located in the village of Hever, Kent, near Edenbridge, 30 miles (48Â km) south-east of London, England. It began as a country house, built in the 13th century. From 1462 to 1539, it was the seat of the Boleyn family.

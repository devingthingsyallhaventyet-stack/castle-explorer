Castles in the Scottish Highlands

The Scottish Highlands are home to some of the most dramatic castle settings anywhere in the world. Scattered across misty glens, perched on lonely lochs, and clinging to windswept clifftops, these fortresses tell the story of clan warfare, Jacobite rebellion, and a landscape that has inspired visitors for centuries. If you're looking for castles to visit in the Scottish Highlands, you're exploring one of Europe's richest heritage regions.

From the iconic silhouette of Eilean Donan — one of Scotland's most photographed castles — to the brooding ruins of Urquhart Castle overlooking Loch Ness, the Highlands offer everything from perfectly preserved clan seats to haunting ruins lost in the bracken. Many of these sites are free to visit, sitting quietly in farmland or on headlands where the only company is seabirds and Atlantic wind.

The region's castle-building story stretches back over a thousand years, from early medieval timber motte-and-baileys thrown up by Norman lords to the stone tower houses of the great clans — Mackenzie, MacLeod, Cameron, and Fraser among them. The Jacobite risings of the 17th and 18th centuries left their mark too: Eilean Donan was shattered by Royal Navy bombardment in 1719, and many Highland strongholds were slighted after Culloden in 1746 to break the power of the clans forever.

Today, Highland castle tours are among the most popular things to do in the Scottish Highlands. Whether you're following the North Coast 500 route past remote ruins or exploring the Great Glen's chain of fortresses, every mile reveals something extraordinary. Many castles double as luxury hotels — Inverlochy, Tulloch, and Aldourie on Loch Ness among them — making it possible to sleep where Highland chiefs once feasted.

Must-Visit Highland Castles

Eilean Donan Castle

Kyle of Lochalsh, Highland

Scotland's most iconic castle, dramatically set on a tidal island where three lochs meet. Destroyed in 1719 and lovingly rebuilt in the early 20th century.

Urquhart Castle

Drumnadrochit, Highland

Commanding the shores of Loch Ness for over 1,000 years. One of Scotland's largest castles, with views that rival any in the country.

Castle Stalker

Appin, Argyll

A tiny tower house on a tidal islet in Loch Laich, famously seen in Monty Python and the Holy Grail. One of the most atmospheric settings in Scotland.

Dunrobin Castle

Golspie, Sutherland

A fairy-tale château in the far north with 189 rooms and formal gardens overlooking the sea. Seat of the Earls and Dukes of Sutherland since the 13th century.

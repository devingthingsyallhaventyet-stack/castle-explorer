Edinburgh Castle 🥾 I've Been
Visited on a rainy Tuesday in March and honestly that made it even more atmospheric. The view from the battlements over the city was stunning even through the mist. The Honours of Scotland exhibition was the highlight — seeing the actual crown jewels up close was incredible. Allow at least 3 hours, we rushed and wished we'd had more time.

Craigmillar Castle 📜 I Know Something
There's a really well-preserved 16th century kitchen in the east range that most visitors miss. If you go through the doorway to the left of the main hall, there's a spiral staircase that leads down to it. The fireplace is enormous — one of the largest medieval kitchen fireplaces I've seen in Scotland.

Dunnottar Castle 👨‍👩‍👧 Family Ties
My great-grandmother was born in Stonehaven and always talked about the castle. She used to say our family name — Keith — was connected to the Earls Marischal who held Dunnottar for centuries. When I finally visited, I found the Keith coat of arms carved above the entrance to the tower house. Quite emotional to see it in person after hearing the stories all my life.

Trim Castle 🕯️ Folklore & Legend
Local legend says there's a tunnel running from Trim Castle all the way to the Yellow Steeple across the river. Nobody's ever found it, but my granddad swore he knew a fella who went in one end and came out the other. Make of that what you will 😄
